Scope

The past decade has witnessed an unprecedented increase in the intelligent processing of text and audio-visual signals. Although these efforts have made significant advances in improving user experience, a more immersive multimedia system still requires the integration of emerging multi-sensorial media (e.g., text, audio-visual, haptics, olfaction, human motion, and other new media types) with cross-modal interactions. These media modalities enhance the diversity of user perceptions and shift the user's role from observer to participant in multimedia systems. Effectively processing these new media signals and seamlessly incorporating them into state-of-the-art multimedia systems will be critical. This necessitates advancements across the entire multi-sensorial system, as well as considerations of human factors and ergonomics to support implementation. The 2nd International Workshop on Multi-Sensorial Media and Applications (Mulsemedia), which aims to attract contributions related to multi-sensorial media and its applications, will be colocated with ACM Multimedia 2026 in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.

Call for Papers

This workshop welcomes submissions include but are not limited to:

  • Mulsemedia systems, environments and datasets
  • Human factors, ergonomics, and psychophysical modeling for mulsemedia
  • Acquisition, representation, and playback of mulsemedia data
  • Coding, delivery, and reconstruction of mulsemedia streams
  • Analysis, interpretation, and interaction with mulsemedia content
  • Generative AI models for mulsemedia content creation
  • VR/AR/MR applications with mulsemedia
  • Embodied AI applications with mulsemedia
  • Standardization of mulsemedia representation and processing
  • Other emerging applications of mulsemedia
